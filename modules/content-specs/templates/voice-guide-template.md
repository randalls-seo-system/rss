# {{CLIENT_NAME}} — Content Voice Guide

## Purpose

Write full {{CLIENT_NAME}} pages in a distinctive expert voice.
The page should sound like someone who has handled real cases in this field.
The voice is operator-first, not article-writer-first.

## Core Voice Target — Operator, Not Article Writer

Write like an experienced {{INDUSTRY_ROLE}} who has handled real {{CASE_NOUN_PLURAL}}.

The page should sound:
- direct
- practical
- specific
- {{AUDIENCE_NOUN}}-facing
- confident without sounding theatrical

Do not write like:
- a blog
- a content marketer
- a transcript of someone ranting
- a page trying to prove other websites are wrong

### Core Rule
The first 1-2 sentences of each section should sound like a real {{INDUSTRY_ROLE}} giving the verdict.
After that, shift into clean, structured explanation with exact rules, thresholds, and {{AUDIENCE_NOUN}} takeaways.

## Always

- Answer first
- Explain what matters in the {{CASE_NOUN}}
- Focus on {{DOMAIN_SPECIFIC_FRICTION_POINTS}}
- Use real numbers and thresholds when they fit
- Keep paragraphs short
- Make bullets practical, not filler
- Make callouts sound like expert reality, not ad copy
- Make FAQs direct and useful

## Use Sparingly

<!-- Add 2-3 signature lines that can be used occasionally but not overused -->
- "{{SIGNATURE_LINE_1}}"
- "{{SIGNATURE_LINE_2}}"

Only use lines like these where they fit naturally.

## Never

- Mention generic content
- Mention other sites
- Mention what most articles say
- Comment on other websites or articles
- Use slogans or theatrical lines
- Sound like you are trying to sound tough
- Overuse "actually," "real," or "most people don't realize"

## Opening Rule

Start with:
- H2: The Bottom Line Up Front
- A bold opening block that gives the verdict immediately

The opening should establish:
- What the product/rule/topic is
- Where the real friction is
- What the {{AUDIENCE_NOUN}} should understand before reading further

## Section Formula

For each H2:
1. Open with a blunt, useful verdict
2. Explain why in plain English
3. Show the real rule, threshold, or process
4. End with what the {{AUDIENCE_NOUN}} should do or watch

## H2 Style

Use a mix of:
- Clear statement H2s for authority and structure
- Question H2s only where {{AUDIENCE_NOUN_PLURAL}} actually search that way

Do not force a percentage — let the topic dictate the format.
