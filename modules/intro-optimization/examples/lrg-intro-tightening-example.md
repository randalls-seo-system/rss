# LRG Intro Tightening — Canonical Example

## Problem

LRG's migrated articles have 4-paragraph intros (120-200 words) with throat-clearing before substance. Readers must scroll past filler to find the answer.

## Solution

50-70 word direct-answer intros. First sentence answers the search query. Disclaimers extracted into callouts.

## Before/After Samples

### 1. Rent-to-Own Homes in San Antonio (post 2662)

**Before (142 words):**
> Rent-to-own can sound like a shortcut, but in San Antonio it is usually a structured plan with strict rules and real risks. The good versions of rent-to-own buy you time: time to build credit, time to save cash, and time to line up financing while you live in a home you may buy later. The bad versions of rent-to-own are expensive leases dressed up as ownership, where you pay extra every month and still walk away with nothing if you miss a deadline or cannot qualify. The disciplined way to approach rent-to-own is to treat it like underwriting in slow motion. You need three things to be true: the contract must be fair, the monthly payment must fit with buffer, and the plan to finance the purchase must be realistic within the option window...

**After (62 words):**
> **Eyebrow:** Updated for 2026. Built for San Antonio renters planning a real path to ownership.
>
> Rent-to-own in San Antonio is a structured plan where you lease a home with the option to buy it later, typically within 2-3 years. The good versions buy you time to build credit and save a down payment. The bad versions are expensive leases disguised as ownership. This guide covers the contract types, real costs, and buyer checkpoints that keep you protected.

### Pattern

| Element | Before | After |
|---|---|---|
| Word count | 120-200 | 50-70 |
| First sentence | Context/background | Direct answer |
| Disclaimers | Buried in paragraph 3-4 | Extracted to callout |
| Filler phrases | 2-4 per intro | Zero |
| Eyebrow line | Missing | Added (8-15 words) |

## Lessons

1. Most "throat-clearing" paragraphs contain exactly one useful sentence — extract it
2. Disclaimers work better as callouts (more visible, less disruptive to flow)
3. The eyebrow line absorbs context that used to bloat paragraph 1
4. 60 words is the sweet spot — enough for substance, short enough to scan
