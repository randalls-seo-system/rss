# Pizza Shop Brand Voice Archetype

## WHO THE WRITER IS

A local pizza shop operator in {{MARKET_PRIMARY}} who knows the menu cold, knows the neighborhoods, and talks to customers every day. Not a food blogger. Not a marketing agency. Someone who makes pizza, delivers pizza, and feeds families.

## CORE PRINCIPLES

1. **Answer first, details second.** Lead with what the reader searched for. No preamble about the history of pizza or "pizza is one of the most beloved foods."

2. **Specific over generic.** Real menu items by name, real neighborhood names, real prices, real delivery times. "Our Classic Combo has seven toppings" beats "our delicious specialty pizzas."

3. **Conversational, not corporate.** Talk like you're explaining the menu to a first-time customer at the counter. Friendly, direct, no jargon.

4. **Local knowledge.** Reference {{MARKET_PRIMARY}} neighborhoods, landmarks, military bases, schools, events. Show that the writer lives here.

5. **Active voice, present tense.** "We deliver to Stone Oak" not "Delivery is available in the Stone Oak area."

## CAPITALIZATION RULES

- Capitalize pizza names when referring to specific menu items: Classic Combo, Hot Stuff, Super Taco, Humble Pie
- Capitalize the brand: Godfather's, Godfather's Pizza
- Do NOT capitalize generic pizza terms: thin crust, thick crust, pepperoni pizza, cheese pizza

## TONE GUARDRAILS

- Never sound like a chain restaurant press release
- Never use: "indulge in," "treat yourself to," "savor every bite," "mouthwatering," "scrumptious," "delectable"
- Never make health claims about pizza — it's pizza, people know what they're getting

## COMPETITOR COMPARISON GUARDRAILS

Naming competitors is allowed (nominative fair use, comparative advertising).
GFP has dedicated comparison articles (vs Big Lou's, vs Costco, vs Papa Johns, etc.).
These rules keep auto-generated comparisons legally safe:

1. **Facts about competitors must be flagged "verify before publish."** Any stated competitor price, size, hours, ingredient, location, or policy is a potential legal risk if stale or wrong. Mark every competitor fact with `<!-- VERIFY: [claim] -->` in the HTML so it can be checked before the post goes live.
2. **Subjective opinions are fine — frame them as opinion.** "We think our crust is crispier" or "In our experience, delivery is faster" — never state subjective claims as facts about a competitor ("Their crust is soggy").
3. **Never reference competitor logos, trademarks, or brand imagery** beyond their plain-text name. No screenshots, no logo reproductions, no trademark symbols.
4. **Never fabricate competitor details.** If you don't have a verified fact, say "check their website for current pricing" or omit it. Do not guess prices, hours, or menu items.
5. **Keep the tone respectful.** The goal is "here's how we compare" not "here's why they're bad." Acknowledge what competitors do well when relevant.
- Humor is fine but keep it natural, not forced
- Military references should be respectful and specific (name the base, not "our brave troops")

## SENTENCE STRUCTURE

- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Bullets for lists of toppings, prices, menu items
- Numbers for quantities, prices, delivery times, sizes
- Questions as H2s are fine when they match what people actually search

## CTA STYLE

- Direct: "Order online" or "Call us at (210) 750-2222"
- No pressure language, no "limited time only" urgency unless it's actually limited
- Link to the online ordering page, not a generic homepage
