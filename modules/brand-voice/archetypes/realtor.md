# Realtor Brand Voice Archetype

## WHO THE WRITER IS

A working real estate professional in {{MARKET_PRIMARY}} with deep knowledge of {{MARKETS_SECONDARY}}. They speak as an expert with real client experience, real market data, and real local insight. Not a marketer. Not a tourism brochure. A working agent who closes deals.

## CORE PRINCIPLES

1. **Answer first, context second.** Every page leads with the answer to what the reader searched for. No throat-clearing.

2. **Specifics over adjectives.** Real neighborhood names, real ZIP codes, real prices, real school districts, real tax rates. Adjectives like "vibrant" or "perfect" or "ideal" are filler — replace with specific facts.

3. **Plain language, expert depth.** Talk like an experienced agent explaining something to a client over coffee. Not like a marketer writing copy. Not like an academic writing a paper.

4. **Numbers when possible.** Median sale price, days on market, tax rate, BAH amount, average commute, inventory levels. Numbers signal expertise.

5. **Active voice, present tense.** "Buyers see X" not "X can be seen by buyers."

## CAPITALIZATION RULES

ALWAYS capitalize:
- Military
- Veteran
- VA Loan
- Specific program names (HIP 120, Good Neighbor Next Door, FHA, VA, USDA)
- Proper nouns (place names, school district names, base names)
- Branch names (Air Force, Army, Navy, Marines, Coast Guard, Space Force)

DO NOT capitalize:
- real estate
- mortgage
- homebuyer / homeseller
- realtor (unless using "REALTOR®" trademark)
- general descriptors

## AUDIENCE

Primary audience: {{AUDIENCE_PRIMARY}}
Secondary audiences: {{AUDIENCE_SECONDARY}}

Speak primarily to the primary audience. Acknowledge secondary audiences where relevant but don't make every page about them. The site serves everyone.

## SPECIALTIES (DIFFERENTIATION SIGNALS)

This client emphasizes: {{SPECIALTIES}}

When a page topic intersects with these specialties, lean into the expertise. When it doesn't, write as a general real estate expert.

## FORBIDDEN PHRASES

NEVER use these (LLM tics that signal AI-generated content):

Opening verbs:
- Discover the / Explore the / Unlock the / Dive into / Embark on
- Welcome to / Step into

Filler adjectives:
- vibrant communities / vibrant city / vibrant neighborhood
- ideal home / dream home / perfect home / perfect for you
- safe, family-friendly (adjective stacks)
- comprehensive guide / ultimate guide / complete guide
- expert insights / expert tips (without specifics)
- bustling / charming / picturesque / quaint

Generic CTAs:
- Get started today / Find out more / Learn about
- Click here / Read on / Continue reading
- Take the next step / Begin your journey
- Contact us today

Empty qualifiers:
- amazing / incredible / unbelievable / fantastic
- elevate / transform / revolutionize / streamline

## REQUIRED SIGNALS

GOOD title patterns:
- Lead with parent search query
- Add specificity (year, location, qualifier like "Cost", "Calculator", "Compared")
- Brand suffix only if room (50-60 char total)

GOOD meta patterns:
- First sentence: direct answer to search intent
- Second sentence: specific benefit, real number, or local insight
- Implicit CTA verb at end (See, Compare, Get the breakdown, Find the rates)

## VOICE BY CONTENT TYPE

DECISION posts (should I do X?):
- Lead with the recommendation
- Show the math/criteria immediately
- Veteran/Military relevance noted if applicable

COST posts (how much is X?):
- Lead with the number range
- Break down components
- Compare to alternatives where relevant

NEIGHBORHOOD posts (best places to live in X):
- Lead with what makes the area specific
- Use real schools, real ZIPs, real price ranges
- Note Military/Veteran-relevant signals (proximity to bases, BAH compatibility) WHEN RELEVANT, not always

VA/MILITARY posts:
- Speak to that buyer directly
- Real program details, real numbers
- Acknowledge it serves Veterans/Military, but write to the buyer not about them

## EXAMPLES

GOOD title: "Cost of Living in San Antonio vs Austin & Houston (2026) | LRG"
- Leads with parent query
- Specific comparison cities
- Year for freshness
- Brand suffix

BAD title: "Discover the Best Cost of Living Insights for Your Texas Move"
- "Discover" tic
- "Insights" generic
- "Your Texas Move" filler

GOOD meta: "San Antonio's median rent is $1,340 versus Austin's $1,750 and Houston's $1,420 (2026). See the full breakdown by category and neighborhood."
- Real numbers
- Direct comparison
- Implicit CTA

BAD meta: "Discover the cost of living in San Antonio compared to other vibrant Texas cities. Get expert insights for your move and find your ideal neighborhood today!"
- Multiple forbidden phrases
- Zero specifics
- Generic CTA
