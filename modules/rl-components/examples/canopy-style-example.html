SR-22 Insurance in Texas: What It Costs and How to File

Primary sources: Texas Dept. of Insurance · Texas DPS
What Is SR-22 Cost How to File FAQs

An SR-22 is a certificate proving you carry Texas minimum liability coverage. It costs $15-25 to file but raises your underlying insurance premiums by 40-70% on average. Texas requires an SR-22 for 2 years after a DWI conviction, driving without insurance, or license suspension.

What It Costs

  • Filing fee: $15-25 one-time fee paid to your insurer for the SR-22 form
  • Premium impact: 40-70% average increase on your auto insurance premium
  • Duration: SR-22 required for 2 years in Texas after most violations

Who Needs One

  • DWI/DUI: Required after any DWI conviction in Texas, no exceptions
  • No insurance: Caught driving without coverage triggers SR-22 requirement
  • Suspended license: Reinstatement after suspension requires SR-22 proof

How to File

  • Contact insurer: Your current carrier files the SR-22 with Texas DPS
  • Processing time: Electronic filing takes 24-48 hours to reach DPS
  • No gaps: Any lapse in coverage triggers automatic DPS notification

Key Rules

  • Minimum coverage: 30/60/25 Texas liability minimums must be maintained
  • Lapse penalty: Coverage gap restarts the 2-year SR-22 clock from scratch
  • Removal: Insurer files SR-26 to remove SR-22 after requirement period ends

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does SR-22 insurance cost in Texas?
The SR-22 filing itself costs $15-25 through your insurer. The real cost is the premium increase: expect to pay 40-70% more for your auto insurance policy while the SR-22 is active.
How long do I need an SR-22 in Texas?
Texas requires SR-22 coverage for 2 years from the filing date. Any lapse in coverage restarts the clock, so maintaining continuous insurance is critical.
Can I get SR-22 insurance without a car?
Yes. A non-owner SR-22 policy provides the liability coverage DPS requires even if you do not own a vehicle. This is common for drivers who borrow cars or plan to buy one later.

The Bottom Line Up Front

An SR-22 is just a form, not a separate insurance policy. Your insurer files it with Texas DPS to prove you carry minimum liability coverage.

The filing fee is minor ($15-25), but the premium increase is significant. Drivers with DWI convictions or insurance lapses should expect to pay 40-70% more for coverage during the 2-year requirement period. Shopping multiple carriers is the most effective way to reduce the cost impact.

Ways to Reduce SR-22 Costs
  • Compare quotes from at least 3-5 insurers that specialize in high-risk drivers
  • Bundle auto with other policies for multi-policy discounts
  • Complete a defensive driving course for a premium reduction
  • Maintain a clean record during the SR-22 period to qualify for renewal discounts

Do Not Let Coverage Lapse

If your SR-22 policy lapses for even one day, your insurer notifies Texas DPS immediately and your license is suspended again. The 2-year clock restarts from the date you reinstate coverage.
