VA Loan Funding Fee: 2026 Rates, Exemptions, and How to Pay

Primary sources: VA.gov · VA Handbook 26-7
2026 Rates Exemptions How to Pay FAQs

The VA funding fee is a one-time charge on every VA loan that keeps the program running without requiring private mortgage insurance. First-use purchase loans carry a 2.15% fee with no down payment, dropping to 1.50% at 5% down and 1.25% at 10% down. Veterans with service-connected disabilities are fully exempt.

2026 Fee Rates

  • First use, no down: 2.15% of loan amount for purchase and cash-out refinance
  • 5% down: Fee drops to 1.50% with a 5-9.99% down payment on purchase
  • 10%+ down: Lowest rate at 1.25% for veterans putting 10% or more down

Subsequent Use

  • No down payment: 3.30% fee on second and subsequent use VA purchase loans
  • 5% down: Drops to 1.50%, same as first use at this down payment tier
  • IRRRL: Streamline refinance carries a flat 0.50% funding fee regardless of use

Who Is Exempt

  • Disability rating: Any service-connected disability rating (even 0%) qualifies for exemption
  • Surviving spouse: Eligible surviving spouses of veterans who died in service are exempt
  • Purple Heart: Active-duty service members with a Purple Heart are exempt

Payment Options

  • Finance it: Most borrowers roll the fee into the loan balance instead of paying cash
  • Cash at closing: Paying upfront avoids interest charges over the loan term
  • Refund available: Veterans granted a disability rating after closing can request a refund

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the VA funding fee for 2026?
For first-use purchase loans with no down payment, the VA funding fee is 2.15% of the loan amount. The fee decreases with larger down payments: 1.50% at 5% down and 1.25% at 10% or more down.
Who is exempt from the VA funding fee?
Veterans with any service-connected disability rating, eligible surviving spouses, and active-duty members with a Purple Heart are exempt from the funding fee.
Can I finance the VA funding fee?
Yes. Most borrowers roll the funding fee into the loan balance. This means you do not pay it out of pocket at closing, but you will pay interest on it over the life of the loan.

The Bottom Line Up Front

The funding fee is the trade-off for having no PMI and no down payment requirement. Most veterans finance it into the loan and never write a separate check.

At 2.15% on a $400,000 loan, the fee adds $8,600 to your balance. That sounds steep, but compare it to private mortgage insurance on a conventional loan: $200-400/month for years until you hit 20% equity. The funding fee is usually the cheaper path.

Funding Fee Exemptions
  • Any service-connected disability rating from the VA (including 0% with compensation)
  • Eligible surviving spouses of veterans who died in service or from service-connected causes
  • Active-duty service members who received a Purple Heart
  • Veterans receiving VA pension based on pre-existing conditions

Retroactive Refund Available

If you paid the funding fee at closing and later receive a disability rating, you can request a refund of the full amount. Contact your loan servicer to initiate the refund process.

The Bottom Line

Finance the fee if you can. If you have any disability rating, you pay nothing.

The funding fee keeps the VA loan program running and is almost always a better deal than PMI on a conventional loan.
