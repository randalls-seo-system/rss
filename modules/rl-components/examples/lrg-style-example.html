Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good mortgage rate right now?
A good 30-year fixed rate today is in the low 6% range for borrowers with 740+ credit scores and 20% down. The national average sits at 6.82%, so anything below that with a strong credit profile is competitive.
Are mortgage rates going down in 2026?
Rates have dropped 12 basis points this week and are trending lower. Most forecasts project rates reaching the low 6% range by late 2026, contingent on inflation continuing to moderate and the Fed cutting its benchmark rate.
Should I lock my rate today or wait?
If you are within 45 days of closing and rates are at or below your target, lock now. Rates can move quickly in either direction, and the risk of waiting often outweighs the potential savings of a slightly lower rate.

Rate Lock Timing

The best rate lock window is 30-45 days before your expected closing date. Locking too early means paying for an extension if your closing is delayed. Locking too late means risking a rate increase.
